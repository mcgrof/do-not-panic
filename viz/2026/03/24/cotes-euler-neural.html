The Near-Miss — 1716

What Cotes actually wrote

ln(cos φ + i sin φ) = i φ
Cotes 1716 · log form · one exponentiation away
① Start from the Pythagorean trig identity: cos²φ + sin²φ = 1 — the unit circle constraint.
② Write the unit-magnitude complex number: z = cosφ + i sinφ , noting |z| = 1 .
③ Cotes observes: the natural log of z must be purely imaginary, iφ , since |z|=1 means Re(ln z) = ln|z| = 0.
④ Result: ln(cosφ + i sinφ) = iφ . He stops here. He never applies exp(·) to both sides.
⑤ The missed step: apply exp → e^(iφ) = cosφ + i sinφ . Euler does this 32 years later.
Euler's Exponentiation — 1748

From Cotes' log to the formula we know

e iφ = cos φ + i sin φ
Euler 1748 · exp form · trivially derived from Cotes
Taylor series proof (Euler's actual method)
e iφ = 1 + iφ − φ²/2! − iφ³/3! + φ⁴/4! + …

= (1 − φ²/2! + φ⁴/4! − …) + i(φ − φ³/3! + …)

= cosφ + i sinφ
The real parts sum to cosine, the imaginary parts to sine — exactly.
Set φ = π :   e iπ = −1 + 0i  ⟹  e iπ + 1 = 0
Euler's Identity — Cotes had this in 1716, latent in his log formula.

The Formula Inside Every Modern Transformer

📡 Attention is All You Need · 2017

Sinusoidal Positional Encoding

Vaswani et al. needed to inject token position into embeddings without learned parameters. Their solution: for position pos and dimension 2i :

PE(pos, 2i)  = sin(pos / 10000 2i/d )
PE(pos, 2i+1) = cos(pos / 10000 2i/d )
Literally: Re(e i·pos/10000^(2i/d) ) and Im(e i·pos/10000^(2i/d) ) — Cotes' formula, split across two channels.

Every original transformer (BERT, GPT-1/2) carries Cotes' phasor as its positional backbone. The frequency 10000 −2i/d is just the θ in e iθ·pos .

🔄 RoPE · Su et al. 2021

Rotary Position Embedding

Instead of adding a PE to the input, RoPE rotates query and key vectors in the complex plane by an angle proportional to position. For a 2D slice of q at position m:

f(q, m) = e imθ · q
Rotation in ℂ by angle mθ — Cotes/Euler's phasor as a position operator.

The attention dot product q T k then only depends on (m−n)θ — relative position falls out for free. RoPE drives LLaMA , Qwen , Mistral , Gemma , GPT-NeoX — basically every competitive open model since 2022.

🌊 Fourier Features · Rahimi & Recht 2007

Random Fourier Features & Kernels

The RBF kernel k(x,y) = e −||x−y||²/2σ² is approximated by random projections using Bochner's theorem: a stationary kernel = Fourier transform of a spectral density. The projection is:

z(x) = [cos(ω j ·x + b j )] j=1..D
cos(·) is Re(e i(ω·x+b) ) — Cotes' real part doing kernel approximation.

Used in NeRF, Gaussian Splatting, and any positional encoding for continuous coordinate MLPs. Also underpins the theory of why sinusoidal PEs work.

⚡ Complex-Valued Neural Nets

Activations in ℂ & Phase-Amplitude Decomposition

Some architectures operate directly in ℂ — representing each neuron as r·e iθ where r is magnitude and θ is phase. Cotes' identity gives the canonical log representation:

ln(r · e iθ ) = ln(r) + iθ
Decomposes complex activation into log-amplitude + phase — exact Cotes form.

Used in MRI reconstruction (k-space is complex), radar signal processing, and complex-valued transformers for time-series. Phase = geometry; amplitude = magnitude — Cotes knew this split in 1716.

Historical Irony

Roger Cotes wrote ln(cosφ + i sinφ) = iφ in 1716. Every transformer you run today — LLaMA, Qwen, Mistral, GPT-4 — uses the exponentiated version of that exact formula to decide which tokens attend to which. Cotes didn't fail mathematically. He just stopped one step early. Newton called him the finest mathematician of his time. He was dead at 33.

Cotes 1716:  ln(cosφ + i sinφ) = iφ   →   stops here
Euler 1748:  e iφ = cosφ + i sinφ         →   applies exp(·) to both sides
RoPE 2021:  f(q, m) = e imθ ·q             →   runs in every competitive LLM
